CATT: From Data to Direction

A Data-Driven UX Research and Design Strategy for a National Health Education Platform

Role UX Researcher & Designer
Timeline 3 months
Team Data Analyst ¬∑ Content Strategist ¬∑ Product Owner
Tools LearnWorlds ¬∑ GA4 ¬∑ Tag Manager ¬∑ Databox ¬∑ Figma ¬∑ Miro ¬∑ Looker Studio

About the Project

CATT (Concussion Awareness Training Tool) is a national public health platform delivering free, evidence-based concussion education across Canada. It serves educators, coaches, healthcare professionals, parents, and youth, each with very different goals and behaviors.

I partnered with The Digital Lab to conduct a data-driven UX audit to understand how users move through learning content, where they disengage, and how the experience could be clearer, more motivating, and easier to complete. (user flow comparison with stakeholder flow)

How might we transform CATT‚Äôs learning experience into a clearer, more engaging, and completion-driven journey?

Phase 1: Research on the Existing Data

As the Data were saved on Google Analytics, LearnWorlds and old platform, we gather them first.

  • Then Audited CATT‚Äôs course structure and user flow.
  • Interviewed stakeholders and public health partners to find out their concerns and prorities.
  • Established KPIs using GA4 + Databox benchmarks to find out design anomalies

Metrics included Qualitative and Quantitative:

User demographics, New vs returning users, session duration, engagement rate , drop-off, funnel flow, and retention rate, click through rate, Page views, Platforms, time in page, scroll rate .

Outcome: Shared success criteria and measurable UX health indicators.

Phase 2: UX Audit in smaller scope

I analyzed 3 months of GA4 data supported by GTM event tracking.

Key Findings

  • 42% drop-off after homepage ‚Üí weak CTA hierarchy
  • Desktop-heavy traffic with inconsistent responsive behavior
  • Broken links and missing back navigation disrupted task flow
  • Popular articles attracted traffic but failed to convert to modules

Phase 3: Insight Synthesis

I connected behavioral data with human motivation.
