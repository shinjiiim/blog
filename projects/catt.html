CATT: From Data to Direction

A Data-Driven UX Research Strategy for Improving Engagement and Module Completion in a National Health Education Platform

Role Design Researcher
Timeline 3 months
Team Data Analyst ¬∑ Content Strategist ¬∑ Product Owner
Tools LearnWorlds ¬∑ GA4 ¬∑ Tag Manager ¬∑ Databox ¬∑ Figma ¬∑ Miro ¬∑ Looker Studio

Project in a nutshell

CATT (Concussion Awareness Training Tool) is a national public health platform that has delivered free concussion education across Canada since 2019. The platform supports educators, coaches, healthcare professionals, parents, and youth through role-specific learning modules.

Problem

A significant gap was identified between the intended user flow and Designed flow . Analytics revealed substantial drop-offs throughout the learning funnel, indicating misalignment between design assumptions and real user paths.

Task

    I collaborated with the Digital Lab team to conduct a data-driven UX audit aimed at understanding:

    How users navigated learning content,
    Where disengagement occurred,
    How the experience could be redesigned to be more motivating & easier to complete.

Method

The project followed a structured UX research process, including planning, mixed-method research, synthesis of findings, and the delivery of actionable product insights through a UX research report.

Result

  • Revealed multiple broken navigation paths
  • Identifying critical friction points contributing to user drop-off
  • Findings and design recommendations were documented and shared with product and content stakeholders to guide platform improvements.

Phase 1: Planning

In the planning phase, I defined project objectives, success metrics, and research methods based on scope and timeline. This included KPI definition, stakeholder alignment, research planning (mixed methods), analytics access review, and requirements gathering.

Phase 2: Research

As the Data were saved on different platforms, gathered and cleaned them.

  • Then Audited CATT‚Äôs course structure and user flow.
  • Interviewed stakeholders and public health partners to find out their concerns and prorities.
  • Established KPIs using GA4 + Databox benchmarks to find out design anomalies

Metrics included Qualitative and Quantitative:

The analysis combined quantitative and qualitative metrics, including user demographics, new versus returning users, engagement rate, session duration, funnel drop-off, retention, click-through rate, scroll depth, and device usage.

Phase 3: Synthesis of Findings

Analysis of three months of GA4 data, supported by GTM event tracking, revealed the following key insights:

Key Findings

  • A 42% drop-off immediately after the homepage, indicating weak CTA hierarchy
  • Predominantly desktop traffic with inconsistent responsive behavior
  • Broken links and missing back-navigation disrupting task flow
  • High-traffic articles that failed to convert users into learning modules
