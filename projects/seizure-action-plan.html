Seizure Action Plan

User Research for Pediatric Epilepsy Management Tool

Role UX Researcher
Project Type Healthcare Research
Timeline 2023
Methods User Research, Clinical Studies

Project Overview

Conducted comprehensive user research for a digital seizure action plan tool designed to help families and caregivers manage pediatric epilepsy. The research focused on understanding the needs of patients, families, and healthcare providers in emergency situations.

Research Objectives

  • Understand how families currently manage seizure emergencies
  • Identify information needs during critical moments
  • Discover pain points in existing seizure management workflows
  • Define requirements for accessible, stress-tested design

Research Methods

  • Interviews: Conducted with parents, caregivers, and healthcare providers
  • Contextual Inquiry: Observed clinical environments and home settings
  • Journey Mapping: Mapped seizure emergency scenarios
  • Literature Review: Analyzed medical research and best practices

Key Findings

  • Families need quick access to critical information during high-stress situations
  • Clear visual hierarchy is essential for emergency interfaces
  • Accessibility for diverse literacy levels and languages is crucial
  • Integration with existing medical records improves accuracy

Design Recommendations

  • Simplified interface with large, clear action buttons
  • Step-by-step guidance for emergency response
  • Multilingual support for diverse communities
  • Offline functionality for reliability during emergencies
  • Integration with emergency services contact systems

Impact

Research insights directly informed the development of a user-centered seizure action plan tool that reduces response time in emergencies and empowers families with clear, accessible information when they need it most.

Skills Applied

Research: Healthcare research, Contextual inquiry, User interviews, Journey mapping

Analysis: Data synthesis, Pattern recognition, Insight generation

Deliverables: Research reports, Design requirements, User personas, Journey maps
