Chapter One

Back to Silence

Nature is my reset. Camping under starlit skies, hiking through misty trails, swimming in cold lakes, or paddleboarding across still water — these are the moments where thinking softens into clarity.

Movement fuels my creativity. Cycling Vancouver's seawall, skiing fresh snow, or wandering with no destination keeps my mind open and curious.

Every place, face, and story is a reminder that people shape their worlds differently.

Chapter Two

Everyday Experiments

At home, creativity takes smaller, everyday forms. I bake sourdough, collect stickers, and treat my nails as tiny design experiments.

And my cats — expert teachers in rest and play — constantly remind me to notice the small joys.

Design isn't just about screens — it's about paying attention to life as it unfolds.
